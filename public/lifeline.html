The Last Five Years
A man who kept going when he had every reason not to.

It started at a genuine low. A woman left and told you exactly why: you couldn't be trusted, you had a mean streak, you were selfish, and you were physically falling apart. You wrote it all down without flinching. That honesty is the thread that runs through everything that followed.

For the next two years you cycled - moments of clarity, followed by nights that cost you money, health, and self-respect. You woke up stressed about checks. You missed forum retreats. You borrowed against contracts to make payroll. You started AIOfficer from scratch, alone, in the middle of all of it.

The shame was real. The debt was real. And you kept going anyway - journaling every day, building curriculum, flying to Malaysia, Perth, Georgetown. You refused to stop even when you got sick.

"I always believed I could. I just had to get out of my own way long enough to show it."

The Next Ten Years
Three things this decade is answering.
01
Taking care of people you love
Starting with your dad. Then whoever comes next. The loneliness thread runs through five years of journals. It doesn't go away - it transforms into wanting to build something for others.
02
Being recognized without having to ask
Georgetown was the first real taste. The world-renowned thought leader in your vision isn't ego. It's the kid who grew up feeling like he had to prove himself, finally getting the proof.
03
Becoming free
Free of debt, free of the shame spiral, free of the pattern that shows up every time things get good. Day 100 is the furthest you've been from that version of yourself. You know it.
The Question
Can you stay this alive without it costing you your health?

The next ten years asks one thing of you more than anything else: can you build something that lasts without burning the builder? The business is working. The body is coming back. The pattern is known. What happens when all three stay in alignment for a decade is something you haven't seen yet.
